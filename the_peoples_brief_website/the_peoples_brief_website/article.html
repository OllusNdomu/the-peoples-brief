Zambia launches new tech hub to boost innovation

By Admin · August 1, 2025

Zambia has taken a bold step towards fostering homegrown innovation with the unveiling of a new technology hub in Lusaka. The facility, developed in partnership with universities and private sector partners, is designed to nurture start‑ups and provide local entrepreneurs with the resources they need to build world‑class solutions.

Supporting the next generation

Speaking at the launch ceremony, the Minister of Science and Technology highlighted the government’s commitment to creating an enabling environment for young innovators. “We want to empower our youth to develop technologies that solve real problems,” he said. “This hub will offer mentorship, funding opportunities and collaborative workspaces.”

The hub features co‑working areas, state‑of‑the‑art labs and event spaces for workshops and hackathons. Organisers say the focus will extend beyond software development to include hardware prototypes and green tech solutions addressing sustainability challenges.

Driving economic growth

Industry analysts predict that the hub could catalyse Zambia’s emerging digital economy by attracting investment and fostering a culture of entrepreneurship. By connecting start‑ups to global networks and local industries, the initiative aims to create jobs and diversify the nation’s economic base.

Members of the tech community have welcomed the move. Local software engineer Chanda Mwila noted that many innovators previously struggled to secure support. “This new hub gives us a place to collaborate and learn from seasoned professionals,” she said. “It’s a game changer for the Zambian tech scene.”

In the coming months, the hub plans to host a series of training programmes and pitch competitions. Aspiring entrepreneurs and students are encouraged to apply for membership through the centre’s website.
